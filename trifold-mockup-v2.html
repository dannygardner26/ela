What Are AI Hallucinations?

Definition

When AI models generate information that sounds confident and authoritative but is completely fabricated—fake facts, invented sources, nonexistent data presented as truth.

How LLMs Actually Work

Large Language Models don't “know” things. They predict the most statistically likely next word based on patterns in training data.

Thesis

AI hallucinations—where models confidently generate false information—are an inherent flaw in how LLMs work, not a bug being fixed, which means users who trust AI output without verification risk spreading misinformation, submitting flawed work, and making dangerous decisions.

Panel C

Why It Happens & What You Can Do

The Sycophancy Problem

LLMs are trained to be agreeable. They'd rather give a confident wrong answer than say “I don't know.”

Leading question
“Isn't the capital of Australia Sydney?”
“Yes, Sydney is indeed the capital.”
Neutral question
“What is the capital of Australia?”
“The capital is Canberra.”

Why This Can't Be "Fixed"

  • Not a bug—it's how prediction works
  • Models have no internal fact-checker
  • Confidence is fluency, not accuracy
  • More training = better writing, not better truth

Protect Yourself

  • Never trust AI output without verification
  • Check every source and citation
  • Treat it as a draft tool, not an authority
  • Ask it to challenge you, not confirm you

“AI doesn't lie on purpose—it just doesn't know the difference between truth and plausible fiction.”
